Community-Led Language Preservation

Build and Preserve Indigenous Language Together

Nunukragang is a community-powered initiative creating accessible digital resources for indigenous languages in Borneo, including a living online dictionary for learners, speakers, and future generations.

About Nunukragang

This project is made by the community, for the community.

Our Mission

Bring together passionate people to collaboratively build an online dictionary for indigenous languages in Borneo.

Our Vision

Create inclusive digital language resources accessible to everyone, everywhere, while celebrating cultural identity.

What We Do

We design practical, community-driven tools to support language preservation.

Online Dictionary Platform

Access an evolving bilingual dictionary contributed to and moderated by native speakers for clarity, relevance, and authenticity.

Community Contributions

Members contribute definitions, translations, and cultural context, helping preserve language nuances that matter.

Multiple Bahasa & Dialect Options

Explore dialects including Lotud and Kimaragang to better understand linguistic diversity and local identity.

Volunteer Opportunities

Open to everyone regardless of background. Native language fluency is welcome, but not always required.

Where You Can Help

Contribute Your Time, Skills, and Voice

Whether you are a student, professional, or community elder, your support helps preserve language and culture for future generations.

Join as a Volunteer

Language Moderators

Review and improve dictionary entries so definitions stay clear, accurate, and culturally respectful.

Program Coordinator

Coordinate volunteer activities, including village trips across Sabah, to collect vocabulary, oral histories, and everyday expressions from local speakers.

Digital Contributors

Support proofreading, data entry, and content organisation to keep the dictionary growing and easy to use.

Media Curators

Curate photos, videos, and storytelling content, including playlist videos for kids learning native languages, to document volunteer work and promote indigenous language awareness.
